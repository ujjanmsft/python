Chicken Choila -Nepali Spicy Grilled Chicken

Chicken Choila is a traditional Newari dish from Nepal, known for its bold, spicy, and smoky flavors. It's typically made with grilled or roasted meat, marinated in a pungent mix of spices, mustard oil, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. While traditionally made with buffalo meat, chicken is a popular and readily available alternative. It's often served as an appetizer or a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or butter (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with mustard oil, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight in the refrigerator for best results.
  2. Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat.
  3. Place the marinated chicken on the grill or in a baking tray. Grill for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char.
  4. Once cooked, remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes.
  5. In a pan, heat ghee or butter (if using) and sauté the sliced onions and slit green chilies until golden brown.
  6. Serve the grilled chicken hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves, sautéed onions, and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  7. Enjoy your Chicken Choila with steamed rice or as a side dish with your favorite Nepali meal!
